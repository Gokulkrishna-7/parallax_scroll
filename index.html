MUNNAR ECO TOURISM

Munnar is a picturesque hill station located in the Western Ghats of Kerala, India. Known for its sprawling tea plantations, rolling hills, and diverse wildlife, Munnar is a popular destination for nature lovers and eco-tourists.

History and Culture

Colonial Past: Munnar was developed as a summer resort by the British during the colonial period. The name 'Munnar' means "three rivers," referring to the confluence of the Mudhirapuzha, Nallathanni, and Kundaly rivers. Cultural Heritage: The region is home to various indigenous communities who have lived in harmony with nature for centuries. Their traditions, festivals, and lifestyle add to the cultural richness of Munnar.
